Forests in Mt.Koya

The first time I went to Mt.Koya. The purpose of this time was greeting Kukai-san and Dake-no-benzaiten-san. Spirits welcomed me at the entrance of the mountain.  

First of all, I visited Okunoin. The place is so nice. It really expanded my sensibilities.
I dedicated my Shakyo(写経) there.  

This is a buddhist rosary that is given as a memento of a donation for saving forests of Mt.Koya. I made a donation at the front desk in Okunoin.
Even though the mountain is crowded with many tour buses and cars, the air is so fresh. The overwhelming strength of forests grown by the local people seem hold the mountain district.
I empathize with local people’s will to live with the nature.

I could hear monk’s preach at a place to rest in Okunoin.
He told me about the following waka. 

高野山　結ぶ庵に袖朽ちて　苔の下にぞ　有明の月

I was moved by the beauty…
I felt sure that I came here to hear this waka.

After visitting Okunoin, I climbed Bentendake from Daimon.
Trees healed my heart and body. It was a blessing.

This time I could have great experiences.
I’d love to go again.
